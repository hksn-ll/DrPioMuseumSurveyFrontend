About the Museum

The Museo Ni Dr. Pio Valenzuela in Valenzuela City was established to honor Dr. Pio Valenzuela, a significant figure in the Philippine Revolution. The museum preserves artifacts, documents, and exhibits that highlight his contributions to the country's fight for independence.

Through its interactive and educational displays, the museum helps visitors understand the struggles of the revolutionary era. It serves as a bridge between past and present, ensuring that the bravery and sacrifices of Philippine heroes remain remembered and appreciated.

Dr. Pio Valenzuela

Dr. Pio Valenzuela (1869–1956) was a Filipino physician and key member of the Katipunan. Born in Polo (now Valenzuela City), he studied medicine at the University of Santo Tomas and became a trusted adviser and physician to Andres Bonifacio during the early stages of the revolution.

He continued serving the country through various roles, including meeting José Rizal in Dapitan in 1896, leading the Katipunan's Supreme Council, and becoming Manila's first Health Officer. Later, he served as Governor of Bulacan, leaving behind a legacy honored today through the museum dedicated to his life and service.
